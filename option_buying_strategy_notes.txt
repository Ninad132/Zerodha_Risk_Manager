Option Buying Strategy Research Notes
Date: 2026-07-09

Candidate strategy:
NIFTY intraday momentum debit-spread strategy.

This is a net option-buying strategy, but not naked ATM option buying. It uses
a long debit spread so risk is defined and theta/IV damage is partly offset by
the short OTM option leg.

Why this is preferred:
- Retail F&O trading has poor aggregate outcomes. SEBI data reported that
  around 91% of individual equity-derivatives traders lost money in FY25.
- Intraday momentum, opening-range breakout, and VWAP-based systems have
  published research support on liquid US ETFs. This does not prove NIFTY
  profitability, but it gives a defensible starting framework for backtesting.
- NIFTY options are liquid and are better suited for automation than many
  single-stock options.

Core rules:
1. Use 5-minute NIFTY spot or futures candles.
2. Trade only between 09:30 and 14:15.
3. No fresh trades after 14:15.
4. Force exit by 15:10.
5. Build opening range from 09:15 to 09:30.
6. Calculate VWAP from market open.
7. Calculate 14-period ATR on 5-minute NIFTY candles.
8. Trade only if opening range size is reasonable:
   - opening range > 0.25 * daily ATR
   - opening range < 0.9 * daily ATR

Long call debit spread entry:
- NIFTY 5-minute candle closes above opening-range high.
- Close is above VWAP.
- VWAP slope is positive over last 3 candles.
- RSI 14 is above 55.
- Buy ATM or slightly ITM CE, around 0.50-0.60 delta.
- Sell OTM CE around 0.25-0.35 delta.

Long put debit spread entry:
- NIFTY 5-minute candle closes below opening-range low.
- Close is below VWAP.
- VWAP slope is negative over last 3 candles.
- RSI 14 is below 45.
- Buy ATM or slightly ITM PE, around 0.50-0.60 delta.
- Sell OTM PE around 0.25-0.35 delta.

Exit rules:
- Stop loss: 35% of debit paid.
- Book 50% quantity at 45-50% profit on debit.
- Trail the rest using NIFTY structure, not option premium alone.
- Exit fully if NIFTY closes back inside the opening range.
- Exit fully if NIFTY closes on the opposite side of VWAP.
- Time stop: exit if trade does not move in favor within 30 minutes.

Risk rules:
- Max 1 trade per direction per day.
- Max 2 trades per day.
- Risk per trade: 0.5% to 1% of capital.
- Daily max loss: 1.5% to 2.5% of capital.
- Stop trading after 2 consecutive losses.

Backtest acceptance criteria:
- Minimum 300 trades.
- Win rate above 52%.
- Profit factor above 1.25.
- Average winner at least 0.9x average loser.
- Include brokerage, STT, slippage, and bid-ask spread.
- Test across different volatility and trend regimes.

Important:
Do not assume a greater-than-50% win rate before backtesting. The strategy is
designed to target that profile, but it must be validated on Indian market data
before live order placement.
